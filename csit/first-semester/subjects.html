C Programming

Core subject

Subject Overview

This course introduces students to structured programming using the C language. Covering fundamental programming concepts, data structures, and algorithms, this course provides a solid foundation for software development.

Course Content

  • Introduction to programming concepts
  • C language fundamentals and syntax
  • Control structures and functions
  • Arrays, pointers, and strings
  • Structures and unions
  • File handling and I/O operations
  • Data structures implementation
  • Algorithm design and analysis

Learning Outcomes

Upon successful completion, students will be able to design, implement, test, and debug C programs, solve computational problems efficiently, and understand memory management concepts.

Course Details

Credits: 4 12 Modules 60 Lecture Hours 30 Lab Hours

Digital Logic

Boolean algebra and circuit design

Subject Overview

This course covers the fundamentals of digital logic design, including Boolean algebra, logic gates, and circuit design principles. Students will learn to analyze and design digital circuits.

Course Content

  • Introduction to digital systems
  • Boolean algebra and simplification
  • Logic gates and circuits
  • Combinational logic design
  • Sequential logic design
  • Memory elements and storage devices
  • Programmable logic devices

Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to design, analyze, and implement digital circuits using various logic components and techniques.

Course Details

Credits: 3 10 Modules 45 Lecture Hours 20 Lab Hours

Mathematics I (Calculus)

Differential and integral calculus

Subject Overview

This course covers the fundamentals of differential and integral calculus, focusing on functions, limits, derivatives, and integrals. It provides essential mathematical tools for engineering and computer science applications.

Course Content

  • Functions and graphs
  • Limits and continuity
  • Derivatives and applications
  • Integrals and techniques of integration
  • Applications of integration
  • Sequences and series

Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to apply calculus concepts to solve real-world problems in engineering and computer science.

Physics

Classical mechanics and thermodynamics

Subject Overview

This course introduces students to the principles of classical mechanics and thermodynamics, providing a foundation for understanding physical systems and their behavior.

Course Content

  • Motion in one and two dimensions
  • Newton's laws of motion
  • Work, energy, and power
  • Systems of particles and rotational motion
  • Thermodynamics and heat transfer
  • Waves and oscillations

Learning Outcomes

Students will be able to apply the principles of physics to analyze and solve problems related to motion, energy, and thermodynamic processes.
